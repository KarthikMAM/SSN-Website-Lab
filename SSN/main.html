History

'Shri Sivasubramaniya Nadar Educational and Charitable Trust' was founded by Mr. Shiv Nadar and his elder brother (Late) Justice Pratap Singh, a Judge of the Madras High Court. A legal luminary, Justice Pratap Singh dedicated his life to a number of social causes that served the greater good of society. The trust is instituted in honour of their father, (Late) Sri. Sivasubramaniya Nadar. The trust has established the SSN Institutions, comprising SSN College of Engineering and the SSN School of Management & Computer Applications. The institutions are run on a not-for-profit basis, and aim to provide the highest quality educational and research facilities for meritorious students belonging to all economic strata.

Scholarships

The unique feature of SSNCE is the scholarships awarded to students. SSN College of Engineering offers attractive student scholarships to encourage merit and to make education accessible to students of all economic strata. The Institution has initiated a thriving tradition of over 250 scholarships offered every year to meritorious and deserving students.

Research

SSN College of Engineering with its vision of being a centre of Excellence in IT and Emerging Technologies established the Centre for Crystal Growth in 2005 to carryout relevant and appropriate R and D and extensive work on this important interdisciplinary area. The Centre for Crystal Growth (CCG) proposes to streamline and co-ordinate research activities in crystalline materials with Academic Institutions, Universities and Research Organisations. The centre aims to provide a pool of Crystal Growth experts to advise and assist students and industrial people in the area of crystal growth and technology ranging from construction to communication industry. The centre will actively involve in collaborative projects with industry and government funding agencies. This includes R&D projects in aspects of crystal growth and technology. Moreover, the centre has identified specific niche areas for further research and development like nonlinear and ferroelectrics crystals, thin film technology for solar energy and photonic devices and various Crystal Growth techniques.
